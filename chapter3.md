# Chapter 3
"No one crosses without paying the toll," Gruff grumbled, his voice like rocks grinding together. "And I don't take gold. I take laughter."

Pip raised an eyebrow. "Laughter, you say? Well, I'm quite the 'comedi-troll'."

He launched into a series of puns, each one more ridiculous than the last. "Why don't scientists trust atoms? Because they make up everything!" Gruff didn't even crack a smile. "What do you call a lazy kangaroo? Pouch potato!" Still nothing. Pip was starting to sweat. He pulled out his wand and with a flick, he conjured a comical image of Gruff wearing a tutu and dancing with squirrels. Gruff burst out laughing, tears streaming down his face. "Alright, alright, you win, little wizard. You can cross."

On the other side of the river, Pip faced his greatest challenge yet: the Labyrinth of Illusions. The walls shifted and changed, creating false paths and dead ends. Pip knew he had to rely on his wits and magic to navigate this treacherous maze. He closed his eyes, focusing his mind, and cast a spell of True Seeing. The illusions shimmered and faded, revealing the correct path. As he ventured deeper, he heard a faint, mocking laughter. Grok, the Gremlin King, was close.
