# chapter 1
In the heart of the Enchanted Forest lived a wizard named Pip, known for two things: his short stature and his love for puns. Pip wasn't your typical towering sorcerer; he barely reached the height of a gnome, but what he lacked in height, he made up for in wit and magical ability. One day, a desperate plea reached Pip's tiny ears. The Crystal Orb of Everlight, which kept the forest safe, had been stolen by the mischievous Gremlin King, Grok, and the forest was slowly succumbing to darkness. Pip, despite his size, knew he was the only one who could retrieve it.

"Well, this looks like a tall order," Pip quipped, adjusting his pointed hat. "But I suppose I should 'rise' to the occasion."

With his trusty wand, a sprig of enchanted willow, Pip ventured into the forest. The trees loomed over him like giants, their branches gnarled and twisted. The air was thick with an eerie silence, broken only by the occasional rustle of unseen creatures. As he walked deeper, the path became treacherous, riddled with thorny bushes and deceptive illusions.

Pip chuckled. "Riddles, you say? Sounds like my kind of 'puzzle-ment'." He approached the blockade, where three Gloom Goblins stood guard, their eyes glowing menacingly.

"To pass, you must answer our riddles," one goblin growled. "What has an eye, but cannot see?"
